PawSquad

A innovative veterinary platform combining digital and home visit services to deliver convenient, personalised and stress free care to our pet patients - day or night.

Problem

Currently, the veterinary relationship is structured around the vet, not the owner. As any pet owner knows, a trip to the vet for a furry friend is about as fun as a visit to the dentist for the average human. Nor is a vet visit fun for pet owners, who have to take time out of their schedules to shift their unwilling pet(s) into a room full of other unwilling animals.

Approach

PawSquad, aims to make a real difference to pet owners' lives by making it easier for them to keep their pets healthy and happy. With the PawSquad app the User can have easy access - whenever and wherever he/she needs it - to professional vets via video and chat consultations, and now home visits. Loved by pet owners and vets alike, PawSquad received the backing of Direct Line Group in 2016.

The challenge is to develop a service that has true value for the end User is to keep pace with the ever-changing market reality that affects the veterinary industry and shapes the User behaviour. To do so, my team adopted from early-on a customer-centric approach to define an efficient and adjustable design process that will help us keep our Users to the center.

Solution

PawSquad combines strong tech and data with the best veterinary team and advisors to shake up an industry that for too long has been lagging in customer-centricity.

PawSquad’s online consultations enable pet owners to speak to a vet via video or text through our website or app without having to travel to a practice. Similarly, the home visit provides pets and their owners with a relaxed and personal veterinary experience in the comfort of their home - making the experience as stress-free as possible. The company’s ongoing relationship with Direct Line Insurance Group also means that the services are reaching more and more of the nation’s pet owners.

My Role

PawSquad currently serves thousands of pets and happy owners in markets like London, Edinburgh, Glasgow, Southampton and Bournemouth, and is expanding rapidly with the aim of becoming the largest veterinary service provider in the UK and beyond.

My main duty is to improve how the user feels when using the product and refactor the site following the brand guidelines.

Other responsibilities

Conducting appropriate user research to improve the UX and the overall feel of the products;

Designing for both web and the print, delivering a service to the User that is aligned with the Brand;

Working closely with the Marketing Team to launch digital and print campaigns;

Adjusting the design as needed to increase the conversion rate and maintain customer retention;

Creating UI (mobile-first) designs for all screen-sizes.

The Design Process

Reviewing - Analysing the collected data to understand how the User is behaving when interacting with our service.

Planning & Design - Assessing the results from the review stage to find out how to address the User's demands and increase User satisfaction & Brand loyalty.

Prototyping - Building prototypes to demonstrate the features of the app and present the storytelling to the rest of the team.

Testing - Assessing the value that the design adds to our service and evaluating the interaction between the user and the interface.

Research

Ensuring that the Company offers what the Users really want is the aim of our User research. A complementary research on all supply chain stakeholders is also needed, since their activities affect the value of the product and the experience the the Customer will get from interacting with the service.

Qualitative Information - to uncover future opportunities, highlight possible issues and see whether an adjustment of the design is needed. Methods: User interviews and online open-ended questions are used to perform the qualitative research and investigate the current service’s strengths and weaknesses, study new User Groups, assess the effect of the service and the campaigns, and understand how the Users are making decisions.

Quantitative Information - to define what exactly are the Customers’ characteristics and their opinions regarding the service. Methods: Online surveys, structured interviews, web analytics and observations to study how the Users interact with the web and smartphone app.
